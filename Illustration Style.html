JOEL · Design exploration

A friendly illustration style
for empty & welcome moments.

The goal is warmth without whimsy — the register Dropbox, Stripe and Intercom land in. We get there with a single monoline system: rounded geometry, generous negative space, and exactly one warm accent per scene. No mascots, no faces, no gradients — those are where “friendly” tips into cheesy. Below: the two surfaces you flagged plus voicemail, each shown two ways.

01

Monoline

One even 2.4px stroke, round caps & joins. Reads as hand-warm but stays crisp at any size.

02

One accent

Ink line art + a single brand hue per scene — blue, AI-purple or green. Never two accents fighting.

03

Objects, not characters

Bubbles, handsets, waveforms — calm props from the product. Skip people and mascots entirely.

04

Air around it

Art sits small (~120px) in a roomy pane. The whitespace is what makes it feel premium.

How it’s built

so it scales to every empty state

Every illustration is assembled from the same primitives — which means new ones (numbers, integrations, WhatsApp) take minutes and never drift off-style.

  • Stroke: 2.4px, round caps + joins, softened ink (#10131a) — one weight everywhere.
  • Accent: a single hue per scene from the existing palette (blue / AI-purple / green), used on one element only.
  • Tonal shape (Dir B): a soft -soft tint behind the art at ~12–16% — adds friendliness without colour noise.
  • Scale: ~112–124px artwork, centred in a roomy pane; copy capped at ~36ch.

My recommendation: Direction B (soft duotone) — the tonal shape is what reads as genuinely warm and Dropbox-like, while the monoline keeps it grown-up.
