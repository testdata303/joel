Clients judge a firm by how it answers.

A prospect calling about a project is deciding whether your firm is responsive and organized. When the call runs through one founder's cell and they are in a meeting, it goes to voicemail and the impression is set.

Client communication should belong to the firm, not one phone. When calls, texts, and voicemails live on personal cells, a partner or assistant cannot pick up the thread, and the relationship walks out the door if that person does.

Business number

Get a new local or toll-free number, or bring the one clients already have. It becomes the number on your site, proposals, and email signatures instead of a personal cell.

ExamplePut one number on the website and every proposal so clients reach the firm, not a founder's private line.

Simple call routing

Set up a short menu and point each option at the right person. New prospects, current clients, and billing can reach different people without a chain of transfers.

ExamplePress 1 for new projects, press 2 for a current client, press 3 for billing.

Shared text messages

Texts to the business number land in one shared inbox. A partner and an assistant can both read and reply, so a client message never gets stuck on one phone.

ExampleA client texts to move a call and the assistant replies from the shared inbox while the partner is presenting.

Voicemail and transcripts

When nobody picks up, the call goes to voicemail. You get the message as a transcript and an email so the right person can read it and respond.

ExampleRead a prospect's voicemail transcript between meetings and reply the same day.

Slack notifications

AnyPhone posts missed calls, new texts, and voicemails to Slack so partners and assistants have visibility and can follow up quickly.

ExampleA missed call from a prospect shows up in the sales channel and the assistant books a call.

Connect the AI answering service you choose

Route any menu option or extension to a third-party AI answering service, not just after hours. Connect it, switch providers, or turn it off any time without changing your number.

Questions

Questions firms ask

Can we keep client calls off personal cells?
Yes. Clients call and text your AnyPhone business number, and you can forward calls to a partner's cell so personal numbers stay private. The relationship stays with the firm.
Can we bring our current number?
Yes. You can port the number clients already use into AnyPhone, or start with a new local or toll-free number.
Can partners and assistants share texts?
Yes. Texts to the business number land in one shared inbox, so a partner or assistant can read and reply without the message getting stuck on one phone.
Can new prospects and current clients ring different people?
Yes. Set up a simple menu and route each option however you like. New inquiries can ring a founder and assistant, and current clients can ring the partner on the account.
What happens to missed calls?
The call goes to voicemail. You get a transcript and an email, and the missed call posts to Slack so the right person follows up.
Is AnyPhone a CRM or helpdesk?
No. AnyPhone is your phone system. It helps calls and texts reach the right person. It is not a CRM, a helpdesk, proposal software, or project management. It works alongside the tools you already use.
