Contractors win jobs by picking up.

When a homeowner or GC calls for an estimate, they are calling a few contractors. Whoever picks up and shows up first usually gets the work.

If nobody answers, they call the next contractor. But you are on a roof, under a house, or driving between sites. Meanwhile current customers, billing questions, and urgent calls all pile onto one personal cell.

Questions

Questions contractors ask

Can I bring my current business number?
Yes. You can port the number your customers and referral sources already call into AnyPhone, or start fresh with a new local number.
Can calls ring my cell while I am on site?
Yes. Forward calls to your cell, the office, a project lead, or an AI answering service. Your personal number stays private because customers only see the business number.
Can the office see and reply to texts?
Yes. Texts to the business number land in one shared inbox, so the office or a teammate can read and reply while you are working.
Can estimates and current jobs go to different people?
Yes. Set up a simple menu and route each option however you like. New estimates can ring you and the office, and current jobs can ring a project lead or teammate.
What happens to missed calls?
The call goes to voicemail. You get a transcript and an email, and the missed call posts to Slack so the office can call the customer back.
Is AnyPhone scheduling or dispatch software?
No. AnyPhone is your phone system. It helps calls and texts reach the right person. It does not schedule jobs, track crews, estimate work, or send invoices. It works alongside the tools you already use for that.
