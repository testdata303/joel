JOEL V1 UI Design Specification

The authenticated product UI for JOEL — a modern business phone system for small teams. Extensions are primary, numbers are entry points, devices are endpoints, and SMS lives in one shared inbox. This document defines the design direction, app shell, and every V1 screen.

Scope · Web application, V1 Source · Product Rules v1.0 · Domain Model v1.1 · Navigation v1.0 Visual system · jack-site (live tokens) No Dashboard in V1
The Mental Model — Never Break This

Numbers in. Extensions decide. Devices ring.

Every screen in this spec serves one chain. The UI must always reinforce it and never demote the Extension.

Voice & terminology guardrailNo telecom language anywhere in the UI. Never “SIP registration failed” — say “Your desk phone is offline.” Never “PBX,” “carrier provisioning,” “trunk,” or “DID.” Banned platform language: communication hub, operating system, unified communications, omnichannel, orchestration. AI is described with concrete verbs (routes, summarizes, transcribes) — never “answers for you” or “AI receptionist.”
Foundations · 01

Design System Direction

The product UI inherits the marketing site wholesale — same tokens, type ramp, buttons, cards, kicker pills, and blue-soft icon tiles. The aesthetic is light, calm, and quiet: Stripe / Linear / Notion clarity. White is the default surface; blue is the only accent; black does the heavy lifting. Nothing here is invented — it is the jack-site system applied to dense application views.

Typography

One body family, Plus Jakarta Sans, across the entire product. Archivo Black is reserved exclusively for the JOEL wordmark in the sidebar. No third typeface. Tracking tightens as type grows; application text sits a notch smaller than marketing for density.

Body & table cells — calm, readable, never cramped.400–500 · 0.95rem · body

Color

The marketing palette, unchanged. Blue is the single accent — resist a second one. Status uses green for live/connected only. The AI violet appears solely on summary blocks, never on chrome.

Status dots, used sparingly● Green = live / ringing / connected / online device.  ● Gray = offline / disabled / archived. No amber, no red dots — errors are plain-language banners, not traffic lights.

Spacing & layout principles

Rhythm

  • 4px base unit; common steps 8 · 12 · 16 · 24 · 32.
  • Generous whitespace over dividers — let air separate groups.
  • Content column caps around 1100–1200px; tables go edge-to-edge inside it.
  • Card radius 14–16px; chips & pills 8–11px; inputs 10px.

Borders & elevation

  • 1px --line borders everywhere; no heavy strokes.
  • Flat by default. Elevation only for overlays: 0 24px 60px rgba(15,17,21,.10).
  • No glows, no gradients (the brand has none), no inner shadows.
  • Kickers above section headlines carry the marketing rhythm inward.

Component style

Every reusable atom is lifted from jack-site. Below is the working inventory the product UI draws from.

Buttons

Cards & icon tiles

Kicker pills & chips

Tables, forms & the Stripe-style settings rail

Forms: stacked label + 10px-radius input, helper text in muted below, inline validation. Settings use a left sub-nav rail (General · Routing · Voicemail · Notifications · Recording · Permissions) with the panel on the right — the canonical Extension layout.

Empty states

Empty is the first run of almost every screen. The pattern is always the same and always inviting: centered blue-soft icon tile, one plain-language line, one short subline, one primary action. Never a dead end, never telecom jargon.

Loading states

Skeletons, not spinners. Reuse the table and card silhouette so layout never jumps. Calm shimmer (no spinner overlays); content streams in. Audio waveforms render a flat placeholder bar until the file is ready.

Skeleton — list view

Error states

The error philosophy is a product rule: plain, recoverable, never technical. Errors describe what the customer sees and what to do next — a human sentence plus a retry. They appear inline where the problem lives, or as a dismissible banner, never a modal wall.

Say this“Your desk phone is offline. Check that it’s plugged in and connected.” · “We couldn’t buy that number. It was just taken — pick another.” · “Slack connection needs attention. Reconnect to keep notifications flowing.”
Never thisSIP Registration Failed · Carrier Rejected (code 487) · Webhook 500 · Provisioning timeout · Trunk unreachable. Carrier-specific terminology is never surfaced to the customer.
Three error surfacesInline field — validation under the input (red text, calm). Banner — persistent “Needs attention” strip for degraded integrations/devices, with a Reconnect/Retry action. Toast — transient confirmation or soft failure, auto-dismiss, with Undo where destructive.
Foundations · 02

App Shell

A fixed left sidebar for primary navigation, a slim top bar carrying the business switcher and global search, and a single scrolling content area. Calm and unmistakably JOEL — the marketing site’s lightness carried into the product. No Dashboard: the user lands directly on their default working screen.

JOEL
Business switcher, top-left of the bar — switch workspaces without logging out.
Global search, centered — searches Calls, Voicemails, SMS, Extensions, Numbers.
Account avatar & in-app notifications, far right.
Sidebar nav — role-filtered; active item carries the blue-soft fill.
Content area — one screen, no Dashboard; lands on the default screen.

Sidebar

The JOEL wordmark anchors the top. Below it, the business switcher, then the role-filtered nav list. Active item uses the --blue-soft fill with blue label and a 2px blue glyph; inactive items are --body with muted icons. Icons follow the lucide-style, 2px-stroke phone-system vocabulary (handset, message, voicemail, hash/number, layers/extension, monitor/device, people, sliders, card). Settings & Billing sit visually grouped at the bottom. Collapses to a 62px icon rail on narrow desktop.

Top bar

Slim and quiet, inheriting the white page surface with a single 1px bottom line. Left: business switcher. Center: global search. Right: in-app notification bell and the account menu. Nothing competes with the content below it.

Global search

Visible on every screen. A click or ⌘K opens a command-style overlay that searches Calls, Voicemails, SMS, Extensions, and Numbers — and nothing else. Results are permission-filtered: a User only sees results for extensions they can access. Grouped results with type labels; plain-language empty result (“No matches for ‘pricing’ in your extensions”).

Business switcher

A user may belong to multiple businesses. The switcher (top of sidebar / top bar) lists every workspace they have access to with its initial tile and name; selecting one swaps the entire context — no logout required. The current business is shown with its mark. This is the only place workspace context changes.

Role-based navigation

Three roles: Owner Admin User. The sidebar renders only what the role can reach. Permissions are extension-based; Inbox access is separate. The default landing screen follows the same rule.

Full configuration access. Billing is Owner-only — an Admin sees the other eight but not Billing.

User

Permission state, everywhereThe UI never shows a locked screen a role can’t use. Out-of-scope nav items simply don’t render. Within a screen, an action the user lacks permission for is hidden, not disabled — except destructive/admin actions on shared objects, which show a quiet “Admins only” note in place of the control.

New in V1 — Forwarding additions

Caller ID on forwarded calls

A per-extension choice in the Forwarding panel — what your own devices see when a call is forwarded to them.

  • Show the original caller’s number — easier to save / call back

Each option carries one plain explanatory sentence. No “CNAM,” no telecom terms.

Schedule-conflict warning

If every enabled destination is restricted by a schedule, the Forwarding panel surfaces a warning so callers never silently vanish.

Warning copy“No active destinations are available during the selected time. Calls will route to voicemail.”

Warning styling, inline at the top of Forwarding. No new scheduling layer is introduced.

No Extension Office HoursDestinations keep their own schedules, but there is no separate “extension hours” layer. Scheduling stays at two levels only: account business hours and per-destination schedules.

New in V1 — four added Settings sections

Four sections were added after the competitive review. All live inside Settings — none becomes primary navigation, and none introduces telecom or enterprise complexity.

Settings → Messaging Compliance

Messaging Compliance

Shows the US SMS (A2P) registration status for each business number, so owners understand why texting may be limited. A table, one row per number.

  • Columns — Number · Type (Local / Toll-Free) · Registration Status
  • Statuses — Not Started Submitted Pending Approved Failed
  • Actions — Start registration · Continue registration · View status
Restricted-state copyWhen a number isn’t approved, say it plainly: inbound texts still arrive (where applicable), but outbound texting is paused until registration is approved. Link the Inbox composer to this section when blocked.
Settings → Hold Music

Hold Music

One business-level setting for what callers hear while a call is being forwarded. Deliberately tiny.

  • Default hold music — selected out of the box
  • Optional uploaded audio — if supported in V1
Not a libraryThis is a single setting, not a greeting library or Voice Studio. No per-extension hold music, no catalog.
Settings → Blocked Numbers

Blocked Numbers

The one place to review and lift blocks added from Calls, Voicemails, or the Inbox. A simple table.

  • Columns — Phone Number · Date Blocked · Blocked By
  • Action — Unblock (per row)
  • Permission — Owner Admin only
Empty state“No blocked numbers. When you block a caller from Calls, Voicemails, or the Inbox, they’ll show up here.”
Extension → Forwarding

Caller ID on forwarded calls

Lives in Extension Detail → Forwarding (closest to where forwarding is configured), not in business Settings. Controls what your own devices see when a call is forwarded to them.

  • Show your business number default — you can tell it’s a business call
  • Show the original caller’s number — easier to save contacts / call back
Explain itEach option carries one plain sentence describing the trade-off — no telecom terms (no “CNAM,” no “ANI”).

Boundaries · 04

V1 Exclusions

What must not appear in the UI. These aren’t backlog stubs to tease — they are absent. No empty “coming soon” tabs, no greyed-out nav, no upsell placeholders. Keeping them out is what makes JOEL feel simple instead of like a stripped-down enterprise PBX.

No DashboardThere is no Dashboard screen, no home overview, no metrics landing. The product opens straight into Inbox or Calls. Do not add summary widgets or a “home” nav item.

Confirmed exclusions (post-review, final)

Decided after the competitive review. Each was considered and deliberately left out of V1 — they belong in future enhancements, never as empty stubs in the UI.

FaxNo Virtual Fax, no fax-to-email, no fax history/tab. Faxing is out of V1 entirely.
Voicemail PINNo dial-in voicemail retrieval and no PIN. Voicemail is read/heard in the app and web only.
Greeting LibraryNo reusable greeting catalog. Greetings are configured inline where they’re used (Settings, Extension).

Not in the product — at all

Wrong category

  • CRM — no contacts as a first-class object, no contact import, lead status, deals, or tags
  • Ticketing — no open/closed status, ticket ownership, or escalations
  • Call-center platform — no call queues, ring groups, skill-based routing, or agent availability/presence
  • AI receptionist / AI agent — AI is analytics only (transcribe, summarize, categorize); it never answers or acts autonomously
  • Workflow builder / automation — notifications are simple event signals, not a workflow engine
  • Advanced analytics — no analytics dashboards, custom reports, or BI surfaces in V1

Out of V1 scope

  • Enterprise telecom — no SSO/SCIM, multi-line SIP, hot desking, shared device pools, or presence
  • Hybrid / smart routing — only Ring-all and Ring-one; no skill or AI routing exposed
  • Nested / multi menus — one menu tree per business; no nested, multi-language, or dynamic menus
  • Per-number / per-extension hours — business hours are business-wide
  • Attended / warm transfers — blind transfer only
  • Shared voicemail boxes, sentiment, AI coaching, campaign SMS — none in V1
  • International numbers, call-tracking pools, dynamic number insertion — none in V1

Navigation & structure boundaries

  • Integrations are not top-level nav — they live inside Settings → Integrations only.
  • Devices are not Extensions — never let a device own routing, voicemail, or notifications in the UI.
  • Numbers don’t get their own inboxes — SMS is one Shared Inbox; numbers are filters only.
  • SIP phones aren’t directly reachable objects — to make one reachable, you create an Extension; don’t expose voicemail/notifications on the device.
  • No per-feature permission toggles — extension access is the unit; Inbox access is the one separate switch.
  • Billing is Owner-only and web-only — never in the Admin nav or the mobile app.

Language & tone exclusions

No telecom terminologySIP, PBX, trunk, DID, carrier provisioning, registration, codec — never surfaced. Failures are plain and recoverable.
No platform / VC languagecommunication hub, operating system, unified communications, omnichannel, orchestration, collaboration layer, “shared conversations” as a concept.
AI stays in its laneAI surfaces only as transcript, summary, and categorization on calls and voicemails. No “JOEL answers for you,” no “AI receptionist,” no autonomous-agent framing anywhere in the UI.
The one-line test for any new UIIf a screen, field, or label can’t be traced to the Product Rules and doesn’t reinforce Numbers in → Extensions decide → Devices ring → Voicemail catches, it doesn’t belong in V1.
