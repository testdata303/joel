JOEL
Design System & Brand Handoff
For use when continuing JOEL design in Claude design or any new context. Summarizes the marketing site we built so the product UI keeps visual and tonal continuity.
 
Status: Reference doc · not final brand book · update as decisions evolve.
1. What JOEL is
JOEL is a modern business phone system for small teams. A single business number with extensions that ring real phones (mobile, desk, landline) and notify modern apps (Slack, WhatsApp).
The mental model — never break this
Business Number → Extensions → Routing → Devices ring → Team notified → Optional integrations
Extensions are the primary operational object. Numbers are entry points. Devices are endpoints. Users are optional. AI is analytics-focused, not autonomous.
What JOEL is NOT
A CRM, ticketing system, or call-center platform
An AI receptionist, AI agent, or autonomous communication tool
An enterprise UCaaS / unified-communications platform
A &quot;communication operating system&quot; or workflow-orchestration tool
2. Positioning & voice
Headline framing
Lead with the category and the audience:
Hero H1: &quot;The modern business phone system.&quot;
Hero subhead: &quot;Everything small teams need to stay connected: business numbers, extensions, texting, voicemail, routing, notifications, and modern integrations — all working together out of the box.&quot;
Closer/CTA band on every page: &quot;The modern business phone system. / Calls, texts, voicemails, and your team connected everywhere.&quot;
AI framing — optional, lightweight, background
AI features must always read as helpful background utilities the user controls. The product is the phone system; AI sits on top.
Use
Avoid
&quot;JOEL routes calls&quot;
&quot;JOEL handles your business&quot;
&quot;JOEL sends voicemails to Slack&quot;
&quot;JOEL learns / notices patterns&quot;
&quot;Optional voicemail summary&quot;
&quot;AI answers for you&quot;
&quot;Helpful follow-up reminder&quot;
&quot;AI receptionist / agent&quot;
Concrete verbs: routes, forwards, summarizes
Personification: &quot;manages,&quot; &quot;watches,&quot; &quot;keeps an eye&quot;
Banned VC / platform language
These words push the product toward the wrong category and must not appear in product UI copy either:
communication hub, operating system, unified communications, omnichannel
orchestration, workflow engine, collaboration layer
&quot;shared conversations&quot; as a top-level concept (Shared SMS Inbox is fine)
Look established / sound professional (dated framing)
Audience
Broad small-business audience: contractors, dentists/clinics, agencies, consultants, local businesses, service companies, and small distributed/remote teams. Avoid founder/startup-specific language.
3. Brand identity
Name & wordmark
Name: JOEL (all uppercase as wordmark; in body copy, also &quot;JOEL&quot;).
Wordmark font: Archivo Black, font-weight 900, letter-spacing .04em, with -webkit-text-stroke 1px (very slight extra weight).
Used only for the JOEL wordmark.
Logo mark (favicon)
Black rounded-square tile with a bold white &quot;J&quot; — the favicon set ships at 32px, 180px (apple-touch), .ico, and .svg.

6. Layout & spacing
Page wrap: max-width container with consistent horizontal padding.
Section vertical padding: 64–90px desktop, ~56px mobile.
Card radii: 14–22px depending on size (small chips/items 11–14px, content cards 16px, hero/feature cards 20–22px).
Borders: 1px solid --line. Card shadow when elevated: 0 24px 60px rgba(15,17,21,.10).
Grids: 3-up card grids on desktop, single column on mobile (≤860–880px breakpoint).
Reveal animation: a .reveal class with IntersectionObserver fades content in on scroll. Carry into the product? Optional — likely overkill for in-app.
7. Core components (already designed)
Navigation
Sticky top nav, 88px tall. Logo left (Archivo Black wordmark). Order on every page: How it works · Features · Integrations · Pricing · [Get your number CTA]. Active item gets a blue underline and slightly bolder weight. Hamburger toggle ≤860px.
Buttons
Primary: solid --blue background, white text, 10px radius, bold weight. Hover darkens to --blue-dark.
Secondary: white background, --ink text, 1px --line border.
White CTA (on blue bands): white background with --blue text.
Card patterns
Feature card: white bg, 1px --line border, 16px radius, icon tile (44px blue-soft square with blue glyph), bold title, body copy in --body.
Plan card (pricing): three plans side-by-side. Popular plan (Grow) is solid --ink (black), white text, translated up by 8px, with blue badge + button. The other two are white.
Quote card: white bg, italic question mark quote (&quot;...&quot;), avatar circle with --blue-soft fill and initials in --blue, name + role below.
Diff card (small): white bg, centered, blue-soft icon tile on top.
Closer / CTA band
Full-bleed --blue band with white text and a white CTA button. Headline + one-line subhead. Standardized across all pages: &quot;The modern business phone system. / Calls, texts, voicemails, and your team connected everywhere.&quot;
Social proof block
Lives on most pages, light --bg-alt background. Stars (5 gold) + &quot;Over 4,000 small businesses served&quot; + INTEGRATES WITH row (Slack · WhatsApp · & more) + three testimonial cards.
Routing / flow visuals
Top-to-bottom routing diagrams use plain white nodes connected by 2px --line stems. Destination cards (mobile, desk phone, Slack, WhatsApp) fan out as a 4-up grid with small pulsing green status dots. Never highlight a middle step arbitrarily — all flow nodes look equal.
8. Patterns we tried and rejected
Hard-won, listed so they don&apos;t come back.
Dark testimonial / social-proof sections — converted to light.
&quot;Look professional / look established&quot; framing — too dated. Use &quot;modern small teams&quot; instead.
&quot;Shared conversations&quot; / &quot;communication hub&quot; / &quot;all in one place&quot; — VC platform language; banned.
Rings/Notifies group row in the hero — felt clunky; replaced with a single &quot;INTEGRATES WITH&quot; row in social proof.
Autonomous AI cards (&quot;JOEL answers in your voice,&quot; &quot;AI receptionist&quot;) — removed entirely.
A highlighted &quot;hub&quot; node in flow diagrams — felt arbitrary; all nodes equal now.
Niche vertical cards (Dental, Contractor, Real estate, etc.) — replaced with six &quot;Built for…&quot; cards (small teams, remote work, modern businesses, speed, flexibility, today).
9. Iconography
Stroke-based icons (lucide-style), stroke-width 2 (2.5 for checkmarks), round line caps and joins.
Inside a blue-soft tile, icon stroke = --blue. On dark bands, icon stroke = light blue (e.g. #9DB0FF).
Sizes: 17–24px for inline/chip use, 21–24px for card tiles, 32–44px for hero/feature tiles.
Phone-system icon vocabulary: handset (phone), mobile rectangle, desk-monitor, Slack hash, WhatsApp chat bubble, sliders (settings), bell (notifications), document (transcript), sparkle (AI/summary), people (team).
10. Content patterns to reuse
&quot;Built for…&quot; verticals
Six cards covering: small teams, remote work, modern businesses, speed, flexibility, today (post-AI era). Use this exact set unless you have a strong reason to change.
Three-step &quot;how it works&quot; pattern
On the homepage, step the customer through: (1) Pick your number, (2) Set up extensions, (3) Start taking calls. Each step is a card with a number, headline, short body, and an icon.
Workflow line
When showing the full system, use the canonical 5-step line:
Customer calls your business number
  → Extensions ring your phones & apps
  → Voicemails & texts stay connected
  → Your team sees updates instantly
  → Optional summaries & integrations, automatically
11. Product-UI handoff — what to build next
The marketing site is done. The next phase is the authenticated product UI, defined by the JOEL Domain Model, Product Rules, Screen Map, Technical Architecture, and Database Schema (all in the project files).
Top-level screens (per Screen Map)
Owner / Admin: Inbox (default if granted) · Calls · Voicemails · Numbers · Extensions · Devices · Users · Settings · Billing
User: Inbox (if granted) · Calls · Voicemails · Settings
No Dashboard in v1.
Default landing: Inbox if user has Inbox Access; otherwise Calls.
Global Search visible on every screen — searches Calls, Voicemails, SMS, Extensions, Numbers (permission-filtered).
Reuse from the marketing site
Color tokens, typography ramp, button styles, card styles, kicker pill, --blue-soft icon tiles, status dot pattern.
The routing visual idiom (white nodes, stems, fan-out to devices) for any &quot;where calls go&quot; UI.
The voicemail card pattern (audio player + waveform + AI summary block + transcript) — already designed; use for in-app voicemail rows.
New for product UI
App shell: sidebar with the screen list above + workspace switcher at top (no logout to switch businesses).
Stripe-style Settings layout (Extensions page is a left-rail sub-nav: General · Routing · Voicemail · Notifications · Recording · Permissions).
Table patterns for Calls, Voicemails, Numbers, Users (no Dashboard, so these list views carry weight).
Inbox: Gmail/Slack-style conversation list on the left, messages on the right.
Forms: empty states, plain-language error messages (no telecom terminology — e.g. &quot;Your desk phone is offline,&quot; not &quot;SIP Registration Failed&quot;).
Things to keep in mind
Extensions are the primary noun in the UI. Don&apos;t demote them.
Recording is OFF by default — make this obvious in setup.
Shared SMS Inbox is one inbox per business. Numbers act as filters, not separate inboxes.
Permissions: roles are Owner / Admin / User. Inbox access is separate from Extension access.
Customer-facing copy never uses telecom terminology. Same rule for the product UI.
