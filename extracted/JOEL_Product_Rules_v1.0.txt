Executive Summary
What JOEL Is
JOEL is an extension-based business phone system that manages itself. It gives a business one or more phone numbers, answers and routes incoming calls, lets staff place outbound calls, captures voicemail and call recordings, consolidates all text messaging into a single shared inbox, and uses AI to transcribe, summarize, and categorize activity. The product is designed so that a customer can be receiving business calls within minutes and can operate without dedicated administrators or telecom knowledge.
Core Architecture
Business Numbers are entry points. A Business Number receives calls and SMS and routes a call either to a Greeting/Menu or directly to an Extension; it never routes directly to a phone, device, or person.
Extensions are the primary operational object. Every inbound and outbound call ultimately belongs to an Extension, and Extensions own Routing, Forwarding, Voicemail, Notifications, and Recordings.
Destinations are the endpoints an Extension rings — a Phone Number, a SIP Phone, or a Mobile Application — and Devices are the underlying endpoints. Voicemail is a terminal outcome rather than a destination.
SMS belongs to the business and lives in a single Shared SMS Inbox; all SMS-enabled Business Numbers feed into it and act only as filters. Notifications are event-driven signals delivered by Email, Slack, Webhook, and In-App. AI analyzes activity and produces transcripts, summaries, and categorization, but is not itself the product.
Product Philosophy
JOEL prioritizes Simplicity, Reliability, Speed, and Low Administrative Overhead over feature complexity. Customers should never need to understand SIP, PBX, carrier provisioning, or any telecom terminology, and failures are always expressed in plain, recoverable language. AI enhances the system but does not replace it; JOEL remains a business phone system that manages itself.
Primary Objects
Business — owns all numbers, greetings, extensions, devices, users, the Shared SMS Inbox, integrations, calls, voicemails, notifications, and analytics. States: Active, Cancelled, Deleted, Restored, with a 30-day recovery window.
Business Number — a business-owned phone number that serves as a public, caller ID, and SMS filter identity. Entry point only.
Greeting / Menu — Greetings communicate; Menus collect input and route callers.
Extension — the primary operational object; owns routing, forwarding, voicemail, notifications, and recordings.
Destination — a Phone Number, SIP Phone, or Mobile Application that an Extension rings.
Device — an endpoint: a SIP Phone or the Mobile Application.
Shared SMS Inbox — the single business-wide home for all SMS conversations.
Voicemail — belongs to the Extension where the call terminates; includes audio, transcript, and summary.
Call & Call Recording — every call belongs to an Extension; recordings include audio, transcript, summary, and metadata.
Notification — an event-driven message to users or external systems.
User — provides access under one of three roles: Owner, Admin, or User. Permissions are Extension-based; inbox permissions are separate. Users are optional.
Integration — business-level connections to Slack and Webhook.
What JOEL Intentionally Is NOT
Not a CRM — no contact management, lead management, or opportunity tracking.
Not a ticketing system — no open/closed status, ticket ownership, or escalations.
Not a call-center platform — no call queues, ring groups, skill-based routing, or agent availability.
Not an AI receptionist or AI agent — AI is analytics-focused only.
Not an enterprise telecom platform — no SSO/SCIM, multi-line SIP, hot desking, or shared device pools in V1.

Product Principles
Extensions Are Primary
JOEL is an Extension-based business phone system. Extensions own Routing, Forwarding, Voicemail, Notifications, Recordings, and Analytics. Business Numbers, Users, and Devices do not own operational behavior.
Business Numbers Are Entry Points
Business Numbers receive Calls and SMS, and route to a Greeting/Menu or a direct Extension. Business Numbers do not own Routing, Voicemail, Notifications, or Workflows.
Users Are Optional
Users exist to provide Access, Permissions, Mobile Application access, and Shared SMS Inbox access. Users do not own Extensions, Business Numbers, or Routing. A business may operate successfully without any JOEL Users.
Devices Are Endpoints
Devices are destinations. Examples include the Mobile Application and the SIP Phone. Devices do not own Routing, Voicemail, or Notifications.
Shared SMS
SMS belongs to the business — not to Extensions, Business Numbers, or Users. All SMS-enabled Business Numbers feed into one Shared SMS Inbox. Business Numbers act as filters only.
AI Enhances The System
AI provides Transcripts, Summaries, Categorization, and Analysis. AI is not the primary product. JOEL remains a business phone system.
Simplicity Wins
Customers should never need to understand SIP, PBX, Carrier Provisioning, or telecom terminology. The system should feel simple.
Feature: Onboarding
Purpose
Move customers from no account to receiving calls as quickly as possible. The primary goal is speed; a customer should be able to start receiving business calls within minutes.
User Experience
Customers choose one of two onboarding paths.
Option A — Just Ring My Phone
Designed for businesses that want the fastest possible setup.
JOEL automatically creates Extension 0 (Operator) and configures it to forward to the customer mobile number. The Business Number routes Greeting/Menu to Extension 0. The customer is immediately operational; no further configuration is required.
Option B — Set Up Extensions
Designed for businesses that want departments, teams, or more advanced routing.
The customer creates one or more Extensions.
Example: 0 Operator 1 Sales 2 Support
For each Extension the customer provides an Extension Number, Extension Name, Text-To-Voice Name, and Initial Forwarding Number. JOEL automatically creates Routing, Voicemail, a Menu Entry, and Notifications.
Business Name
The customer enters a Business Name (example: Smilebar), used by Greetings, Voicemail Greetings, and generated audio.
Number Acquisition
During onboarding the customer chooses to Buy Number or Port Existing Number.
Temporary Numbers
Customers porting numbers automatically receive a temporary number. Porting never blocks onboarding. Customers may configure Extensions, Greetings, Routing, and Notifications, invite Users, and test the system before the port completes.
Default Configuration
New businesses default to:
Business Hours: 24/7
Directory: Enabled
Greeting: Automatically Generated
Voicemail: Enabled
Email Notifications: Enabled
Completion
Onboarding is complete when a Business Number exists, a Greeting exists, an Extension exists, and a Destination exists. The customer can then immediately receive calls.
Feature: Business Numbers
Purpose
Business Numbers provide Public Identity, Caller ID Identity, and SMS Identity. Business Numbers are entry points, not operational objects. Business Numbers do not own Routing, Voicemail, Notifications, or Workflows.
User Experience
A business may own multiple Business Numbers.
Example: Main Number, Marketing Number, Google Ads Number, Support Number.
All Business Numbers belong to the same business.
Number Types
Local Numbers
Associated with geographic areas (examples: 617, 212, 305).
Toll-Free Numbers
Examples: 800, 888, 877, 866, 855, 844, 833.
Number Purchase
The customer chooses Local Number, Toll-Free Number, or Port Existing Number. All number acquisition begins from the same workflow.
Local Number Search
The customer searches by City or State. The area code is displayed for clarity; customers are not expected to know area codes.
Example: Boston, MA (617)
JOEL displays 3 suggested numbers with a Show More option for additional choices, to reduce decision fatigue.
Toll-Free Search
The customer may generate available numbers, select a preferred prefix, or search vanity numbers (examples: 1-800-SMILEBAR, 1-800-FLOWERS).
Number Activation
After purchase, the Business Number becomes active immediately. Default routing is the Main Greeting/Menu unless the customer explicitly chooses otherwise.
Additional Numbers
Additional Business Numbers may be purchased at any time as add-ons. Customers are not required to upgrade plans solely to add another number.
Routing
A Business Number may route to a Main Greeting/Menu or a Direct Extension.
Example: Main Number to Greeting/Menu; Sales Number to Extension 1 (Sales).
Business Numbers never route directly to Phone Numbers, SIP Phones, or Mobile Applications. Business Numbers route only to a Greeting/Menu or an Extension.
SMS Behavior
All SMS-enabled Business Numbers feed into the Shared SMS Inbox. Business Numbers act as filters only and do not receive independent inboxes.
Caller ID Identity
Business Numbers serve as outbound caller ID identities. Outbound calls always display a business-owned number. Caller ID selection follows Device configuration, Extension permissions, and administrator restrictions. (See Users & Permissions for allowed caller ID rules.)
Notifications
Business Numbers do not own Notifications. Notifications belong to Extensions or the Shared SMS Inbox depending on event type.
Number Porting
Purpose
Allow customers to move existing Business Numbers into JOEL without interrupting onboarding.
Port Existing Number
The customer enters the existing number, current carrier, ownership information, and required documentation. Porting begins immediately.
Temporary Number
JOEL automatically assigns a temporary number so the customer may configure Extensions, Greetings, Routing, and Notifications, invite Users, and test the system while the port is in progress. Temporary numbers cannot be skipped.
Port Wizard
JOEL guides the customer through Carrier Selection, Ownership Verification, Documentation Requirements, and Port Authorization, to minimize support involvement.
Port Status
Customers can view status at all times: Submitted, Review, Approved, Scheduled, Completed.
Port Completion
When the port completes, the temporary number is automatically replaced with the ported number. All routing and configuration remains intact; no reconfiguration is required.
Port Failure
The customer receives Additional Information Required or Carrier Rejected Request, with next steps. Customers never see carrier-specific technical terminology.
Number Deletion
Deleting a Business Number requires confirmation. (Future MFA confirmation may be supported.)
Recovery Window
Deleted Business Numbers enter a recovery period and are not immediately released. Administrators may restore deleted numbers during the recovery period.
Deleted Numbers
Historical records remain available.
Example: Deleted Number — Google Ads Number 617-555-1212.
Retained historical data: Calls, SMS, Voicemails, Recordings, Transcripts, Summaries, Analytics.
Number Restoration
Restoring a deleted Business Number restores Routing, Greetings, SMS, and historical references. No reconfiguration is required.
Number Reuse
Business Numbers are not immediately released; the recovery period must expire first.
Analytics
Business Numbers provide attribution grouping (examples: Main Number, Marketing Number, Google Ads Number). Future reporting may group Calls, SMS, and Voicemails by number.
Audit Log
Recorded: Number Purchased, Number Deleted, Number Restored, Port Submitted, Port Approved, Port Completed, Routing Changed.
Future Enhancements
Not V1: International Numbers, Dynamic Number Insertion, Call Tracking Pools, Shared Numbers Across Businesses, Advanced Marketing Attribution.
Feature: Extensions
Purpose
Extensions are the primary operational object inside JOEL. Every inbound and outbound call ultimately belongs to an Extension (examples: Sales, Support, Billing, Emergency, Bob, Front Desk). Extensions own operational behavior.

Standard Extensions

Additional Extensions
Additional Extensions begin at 100 (100, 101, 102, …). No shortcut mapping layer exists in V1. Menu selections route directly to Extension numbers.
Example: Press 1 to Extension 1; Press 0 to Extension 0 (Operator); Press 9 to Directory.
Extension Creation
The customer provides Extension Number, Extension Name, Text-To-Voice Name, and Initial Forwarding Number.
Example: Extension: 1 Name: Sales Text-To-Voice Name: Sales Forward To: 617-555-1111

Permissions

Example: A user with access to Sales automatically receives access to Sales Calls, Sales Voicemails, Sales Recordings, Sales Forwarding, Sales Notifications, and Sales Outbound Calling.

Feature: Greetings & Menu
Purpose
Greetings and Menus define the caller experience before a call reaches an Extension. Greetings communicate information. Menus collect caller input and route callers. Greetings and Menus are separate concepts.
Greeting Responsibilities
Greetings provide Welcome Messages, Business Information, Closed Notices, Holiday Notices, Temporary Closure Notices, Transfer Messages, Voicemail Messages, and Recording Announcements. Greetings do not perform routing and do not own Extensions or Destinations.
Menu Responsibilities
Menus provide Digit Routing, Timeout Handling, Invalid Input Handling, and Directory Access. Menus own routing behavior.
User Experience
The customer configures a Greeting and a Menu.
Example — Greeting: Thank you for calling Smilebar. For Sales press 1. For Support press 2. For our directory press 9.
Example — Menu: 1 to Sales 2 to Support 0 to Operator 9 to Directory
The Greeting is what callers hear. The Menu determines what happens when callers respond.
Greeting Types
Main Greeting
Default business greeting.
Example: Thank you for calling Smilebar.
After-Hours Greeting
Example: Thank you for calling Smilebar. Our office is currently closed.
Holiday Greeting
Example: Thank you for calling Smilebar. We are closed today for Memorial Day.
Temporary Closure Greeting
Example: Thank you for calling Smilebar. We are temporarily closed and will reopen on July 7.
Voicemail Greeting
Example: Sorry we missed your call. Please leave a message after the tone.
After-Hours Voicemail Greeting
Example: We are currently closed. Please leave a message and we will return your call.
Transfer Greeting
Example: Please wait while we connect you to Sales.
Recording Announcement
Example: This call may be recorded for quality and training purposes.
Greeting Creation
AI-Generated Audio
Default behavior and primary V1 method. The customer enters Greeting Text and selects a Voice; JOEL automatically generates the audio. The customer may Preview Audio, Change Voice, and Edit Text.
Uploaded Audio
Supported as an advanced option, used when customers require custom recordings.
Text-To-Voice Names
Every Extension supports two names:
Extension Name
Internal display name (example: Dr. Niaraki).
Text-To-Voice Name
Used by Greetings, the Directory, and Call Screening (example: Doctor Niaraki). The Text-To-Voice Name does not need to match the Extension Name; this allows proper pronunciation.
Menu Structure
One menu tree per business in V1.
Example: 0 to Operator 1 to Sales 2 to Support 3 to Billing 9 to Directory
Menu Creation
Menu entries are automatically created when Extensions are created.
Example: Creating Extension 1 (Sales) automatically creates the menu entry 1 to Sales.
Menu Updates
If an Extension name changes (example: Sales to Appointments), menu routing remains unchanged and AI-generated Greetings update automatically.
Menu Deletion
If an Extension is deleted (example: Extension 2 Support), the menu option 2 is automatically removed. Broken menu entries are never left behind.
Directory Integration
By default the Directory is enabled at 9. If disabled, 9 follows Invalid Input Behavior.
No Input Behavior
Configurable. Supported actions: Replay Greeting, Route To Extension, Disconnect. Recommended default: Replay Greeting once, then Route To Extension 0.
Invalid Input Behavior
Configurable. Supported actions: Replay Greeting, Route To Extension, Disconnect. Recommended default: Replay Greeting once, then Route To Extension 0.
Business Hours Interaction
Business Hours do not modify menu behavior. The same Menu is presented whether the business is Open or Closed; only the Greeting differs.
Permissions
Only authorized users may Edit Greetings, Edit Menus, and Enable or Disable the Directory. Admins always have access.
Future Enhancements
Not V1: Multiple Menu Trees, Nested Menus, Multi-Language Menus, Dynamic Menus, AI Menu Recommendations, Speech-Only Menus.
Feature: Directory
Purpose
The Directory allows callers to locate Extensions by name, reducing menu complexity and allowing callers to reach the correct Extension without knowing the Extension number. The Directory is accessed through 9 by default.
User Experience
The caller hears “For our directory, press 9,” presses 9, and the Directory begins.
Search Methods
Voice Search
Supported (examples: Sales, Support, Bob, Jane).
Dial-By-Name
Supported. The caller enters letters using standard telephone keypad mapping (example: BOB).
Search Scope
The Directory searches Extension Names only (examples: Sales, Support, Billing, Bob, Jane). The Directory does not search Phone Numbers, SIP Phone names, Users without Extensions, or internal system objects.
Ambiguous Results
Example: For matches Sales, Sales Team, and Sales Manager — “I found three matches. For Sales press 1. For Sales Team press 2. For Sales Manager press 3.”
The caller selects the desired Extension.
No Match Found
The Directory responds “I couldn’t find a match. Please try again.” The caller may search again or use Dial-By-Name.
Excluded Extensions
Disabled Extensions and Deleted Extensions do not appear in the Directory.
Permissions
The Directory is public. No user permissions apply.
Future Enhancements
Not V1: Natural Language Requests, Multi-Language Directory, Fuzzy Matching, Caller Personalization, AI Recommendations.
Feature: Business Hours
Purpose
Business Hours determine which Greeting is played to callers. Business Hours are intentionally lightweight and are not a routing engine. Business Hours do not control Extensions, Destinations, Voicemail, or Notifications. (Forwarding schedules belong to Destinations.)
User Experience
Customers may choose Always Play Main Greeting or Use Business Hours.
Example: Open — Main Greeting; Closed — After-Hours Greeting.
Default Behavior
New businesses default to 24/7; no configuration is required.
Settings
Time Zone
Used for Greeting selection (example: Eastern Time).
Weekly Schedule
Example: Monday 9AM–5PM, Tuesday 9AM–5PM.
Holiday Schedule
Supported in V1 (example: Memorial Day triggers the Holiday Greeting).
Temporary Closure
Example: July 1 – July 7 triggers the Temporary Closure Greeting.
Greeting Selection
Open: Play Main Greeting
Closed: Play After-Hours Greeting
Holiday: Play Holiday Greeting
Temporary Closure: Play Temporary Closure Greeting
Menu Behavior
Business Hours do not modify Menus. The Menu is identical whether Open or Closed; only the Greeting differs.
Forwarding Schedules
Forwarding schedules belong to Destinations and are independent of Business Hours.
Example: Sales — Forward To 617-555-1111, Schedule Monday–Friday 9AM–5PM.
Permissions
Only authorized users may modify Business Hours, Holiday Schedules, and Temporary Closures. Admins always have access.
Audit Log
Recorded: Business Hours Changed, Holiday Added, Holiday Removed, Temporary Closure Added, Temporary Closure Removed.
Future Enhancements
Not V1: Per-Number Hours, Per-Extension Hours, AI Schedule Suggestions, Automatic Holiday Detection.
Feature: Routing
Purpose
Routing determines how an Extension delivers calls to configured Destinations. Routing belongs entirely to the Extension. Routing does not belong to Business Numbers, Devices, or Users.
Routing Methods
Ring All At Once
All enabled Destinations ring simultaneously.
Example: Sales rings Bob Mobile, Jane Mobile, and Front Desk SIP Phone together.
Ring One At A Time
Destinations ring according to configured order.
Example: Sales attempts Bob Mobile, then Jane Mobile, then Front Desk SIP Phone, then Voicemail — each in sequence.
Hybrid routing is not exposed in V1.
Fallback Behavior
When all Destinations fail, the Extension follows its configured fallback behavior:
Voicemail
Most common. The caller reaches the Extension Voicemail.
Disconnect
The caller hears a disconnect message and the call ends.
Forward To Extension
Example: Sales forwards to Support; the caller is transferred to another Extension.
Loop Prevention
Routing loops are not permitted. JOEL prevents saving invalid configurations.
Example: Sales to Support and Support to Sales is rejected.
Future Enhancements
Not V1: Call Queues, Ring Groups, Skill-Based Routing, Agent Availability, Workflow Routing, AI Routing.
Feature: Destinations
Purpose
Destinations are the endpoints an Extension can ring. Extensions own Destination assignments. Devices exist independently.
Supported Destination Types
Phone Number (example: 617-555-1111)
SIP Phone (example: Front Desk)
Mobile Application (example: Jane Mobile Application)
Destination Creation
The customer clicks Add Destination, then chooses Phone Number, SIP Phone, or Mobile Application.
Destination Settings
Every Destination supports:
Enabled (On / Off)
Schedule (example: Monday–Friday 9AM–5PM)
Ring Duration (advanced setting, hidden during basic setup)
Order (used only for Ring One At A Time)
Call Handling
Direct Connect
Default behavior. The call immediately connects with no announcement and no screening.
Call Screening
Optional Destination-level setting. When enabled, the recipient hears: “JOEL call for Sales. Press 1 to accept. Press 2 to send to voicemail. Press 3 to continue routing.” The caller hears hold music while waiting.
The announcement uses the Extension’s Text-To-Voice Name (examples: “JOEL call for Sales,” “JOEL call for Support,” “JOEL call for Doctor Niaraki”). Caller name and caller number are not announced in V1.
Screening outcomes:
Press 1 — Accept Call: the call immediately connects.
Press 2 — Send To Voicemail: the caller is routed to the Extension Voicemail.
Press 3 — Continue Routing: the call proceeds to the next Destination.
Timeout — Continue Routing: the call proceeds automatically.
Destination Removal
Removing a Destination stops future routing and preserves historical Calls, Recordings, and Voicemails.
Disabled Destinations
Disabled Destinations behave as though they do not exist; no special routing occurs.
Device Requirements
A Mobile Application Destination requires an existing User.
A SIP Phone may be attached to multiple Extensions.
A Mobile Application may be attached to multiple Extensions.
Example: Sales to Front Desk, Support to Front Desk, Operator to Front Desk — all referencing the same SIP Phone.
Future Enhancements
Not V1: Shared Device Pools, Presence, Agent Availability, Smart Routing.
Feature: Devices

Supported Device Types

Physical office phone (examples: Front Desk, Reception, Conference Room).
Mobile Application
User-operated device (examples: iPhone, Android).
Future Softphone
Not part of V1.
Device Management

Device Settings
Device Name — human-friendly label (example: Front Desk).
Device Type — SIP Phone or Mobile Application.
Status — Active or Disabled.
Default Outbound Extension (example: Operator (0), Sales (1)).
Default Caller ID Number (example: Main Number, Sales Number), used for outbound caller ID presentation.
SIP Phones
Administration

Provisioning

Outbound Behavior

Example: Sales to Front Desk, Support to Front Desk, Operator to Front Desk.

Feature: Mobile Application
Purpose
The Mobile Application allows Users to operate JOEL while away from a desk. The Mobile Application is an operational tool, not an administration tool.
Primary Functions
Users may Make Outbound Calls, Receive Inbound Calls, View Call History, Listen To Voicemails, View Voicemail Transcripts, View Voicemail Summaries, manage SMS conversations in the Shared SMS Inbox, and Switch Organizations.
Navigation

Unsupported Functions

Feature: Calls
Purpose
Calls are the inbound and outbound voice interactions handled by JOEL. Every call ultimately belongs to an Extension, which determines how the call is owned, permitted, recorded, and grouped for analytics.
Inbound Calls
An inbound call arrives on a Business Number, which routes it to a Greeting/Menu or directly to an Extension. The Extension then delivers the call to its configured Destinations according to its routing method. An inbound call is associated with the Business, the Business Number that received it, and the Extension that handles it.
Outbound Calls
A User places an outbound call through an Extension they have access to, using a Caller ID Number allowed for that Extension. The call always displays a business-owned number. An outbound call is associated with the Business, the Extension, the Caller ID Number presented, and the Device or User that placed it.
Call Ownership
Every inbound and outbound call belongs to an Extension. The Extension determines ownership, permissions, recording, and analytics. Business Numbers, Devices, and Users do not own calls.
Call History
Call history is available in the Web Application and the Mobile Application. Users may view Answered Calls, Missed Calls, Recorded Calls, and Call Details. Call visibility follows Extension permissions; Users only see calls belonging to Extensions they can access.
Missed Calls
A call is missed when it reaches an Extension, no Destination answers, and the caller disconnects without leaving a voicemail. Missed calls appear in call history and may trigger the Missed Call notification.
Call Metadata
Each call includes Caller Number, Date/Time, Extension, Duration, Direction, and Caller ID Number.
Search
Calls are searchable — Answered Calls, Missed Calls, and Recorded Calls — by Caller Number, Extension, and Date. Search results respect permissions, so Users only see calls for Extensions they can access.
Permissions
Access to an Extension grants access to that Extension’s calls, including call history, call details, and recordings. No separate per-call permissions exist in V1; permissions inherit from Extension access.
Notifications
Call-related notification events are Missed Call (Email, Slack, Webhook, In-App) and Call Completed (Webhook). Notifications belong to the Extension.
Audit Log
The Audit Log records configuration changes only; call activity such as placing, receiving, or viewing a call is not recorded. Configuration changes that affect call handling — for example routing and number changes — are recorded under their respective objects.
Future Enhancements
Not V1: Call Queues, Ring Groups, Skill-Based Routing, Agent Availability.
Feature: Call Transfers
Purpose
Call Transfers move a call from one Extension to another, or to voicemail, using the Extension’s configured routing behavior. V1 supports blind transfers only.
Blind Transfer
A blind transfer passes the call to the target without consultation and is the only transfer type in V1. After the transfer, the call follows the target Extension’s routing and Destinations. If recording is enabled, recording continues across the transfer and the entire call remains recorded.
Transfer To Extension
A call may be transferred to another Extension, after which it follows that Extension’s routing and Destinations. A Transfer Greeting may be played while connecting (for example, “Please wait while we connect you to Sales”).
Example: Sales → Support — the caller is transferred to the Support Extension.
Transfer To Voicemail
When a transferred call is not answered, it reaches the voicemail of the Extension where the call terminates. Voicemail belongs to that final Extension; the original route is stored for historical purposes.
Example: Sales → Support → Support Voicemail — the voicemail belongs to Support.
Transfer Failure
If all Destinations of the receiving Extension fail, that Extension follows its configured fallback behavior — Voicemail, Disconnect, or Forward To Extension. Routing loops are not permitted; JOEL prevents saving configurations that would create a transfer loop.
Transfer To Disabled Extension
A disabled Extension behaves as though it does not exist for routing purposes and does not appear in the Directory. It cannot serve as a transfer target; the call instead follows the originating Extension’s fallback behavior.
Transfer To Deleted Extension
A deleted Extension is removed from menus and the Directory, and broken menu entries are never left behind, so a deleted Extension cannot be a transfer target. Historical calls, recordings, and voicemails associated with the deleted Extension remain available and searchable.
Permissions
Transfers follow Extension permissions. A User may transfer calls only within Extensions they have access to. No separate transfer permission exists in V1.
Audit Log
The Audit Log records configuration changes only; individual call transfers are call activity and are not recorded. Changes to forwarding and routing configuration are recorded under Routing events.
Future Enhancements
Not V1: Attended Transfer, Warm Transfer, Consultative Transfer.
Feature: Voicemail
Purpose
Voicemail allows callers to leave a message when an Extension is unable to answer. Voicemail belongs to the Extension where the call terminates. Voicemail does not belong to Business Numbers, Devices, or Users.
User Experience
The caller reaches an Extension (example: Sales). No Destination answers. The caller hears the Sales Voicemail Greeting and leaves a message. JOEL creates Audio, a Transcript, and a Summary, and Notifications are sent. The voicemail appears in the Extension’s voicemail area.
Ownership
Voicemail belongs to the final Extension.
Example: Sales to Support to Support Voicemail — the voicemail belongs to Support. The original route remains stored for historical purposes.
Voicemail Contents
Every voicemail includes:
Audio — original recording.
Transcript — generated automatically.
Summary — generated automatically.
Metadata — Caller Number, Date/Time, Extension, Original Route, Final Route, Duration.
Voicemail Greetings
Voicemail Greeting (example: Sorry we missed your call. Please leave a message after the tone.)
After-Hours Voicemail Greeting (example: We are currently closed. Please leave a message and we will return your call.)
Greetings are produced using AI-Generated Audio or Uploaded Audio (see Greetings & Menu).
Voicemail Inbox
A dedicated voicemail area, separate from Call History and the Shared SMS Inbox. Supports Search, Filter, and Playback.
Notifications
A New Voicemail event notifies via Email, Slack, Webhook, and In-App. (See Feature: Notifications for channel behavior.)
Permissions
Users with access to an Extension may View Voicemails, Listen To Audio, View Transcript, and View Summary. No separate voicemail permissions exist in V1.
Search
Voicemail search supports Caller Number, Transcript Content, Summary Content, and Extension Name.
Example: Searching “pricing” returns voicemails discussing pricing.
Deletion
Deleting a voicemail permanently removes Audio, Transcript, and Summary. Confirmation is required.
Retention
Manual deletion and retention expiry are distinct. When retention expires, JOEL deletes the Audio and retains the Transcript, Summary, and Metadata. This preserves business value while reducing storage costs.
Deleted Extensions
If an Extension is deleted, historical voicemails remain available and searchable.
Example: Deleted Extension — Sales (1). Voicemails remain searchable.
Blocked Callers
Blocked callers cannot leave new voicemails. Existing voicemail history remains available.
Future Enhancements
Not V1: Shared Voicemail Boxes, Voicemail Assignment, Internal Notes, AI Callback Suggestions, Sentiment Analysis.
Feature: Call Recording
Purpose
Call Recording captures call audio and generates derived artifacts when recording is enabled, regardless of call direction.
Recorded Calls
For both Inbound and Outbound calls, when recording is enabled JOEL creates an Audio Recording, a Transcript, and a Summary.
Transfers
Recording continues across transfers and does not stop during transfer.
Example: Sales (recorded) to Support — the entire call remains recorded.
Recording Contents
Each recording contains:
Audio — original call recording.
Transcript — automatically generated.
Summary — automatically generated (example: “Customer called regarding pricing and requested a callback.”).
Metadata — Caller Number, Date/Time, Extension, Duration, Direction, Caller ID Number.
Permissions
Users with access to an Extension may Listen To Recordings, View Transcripts, and View Summaries. No separate recording permissions exist in V1.
Search
Recordings are searchable by Caller Number, Transcript Content, Summary Content, Extension, and Date.
Downloads
Authorized users may download the Audio Recording when permitted.
Retention
When retention expires, JOEL deletes the Audio and retains the Transcript, Summary, and Metadata. This preserves business value while reducing storage costs.
Deleted Extensions
If an Extension is deleted, historical recordings remain available and searchable.
Example: Deleted Extension — Sales (1). Recordings remain searchable.
Audit Log
Recorded: Recording Enabled, Recording Disabled, Retention Changed.
Future Enhancements
Not V1: Pause Recording, Speaker Identification, Sentiment Analysis, AI Coaching, Automatic Redaction.
Feature: Notifications
Purpose
Notifications inform users and external systems about important events occurring inside JOEL and reduce the need to log into JOEL. Notifications are event-driven. Notifications are not workflows and are not automation.
Ownership
Notifications belong to Extensions and to the Shared SMS Inbox. Notifications do not belong to Business Numbers, Devices, individual Calls, or individual Voicemails.
Notification Channels
Email (example: sales@company.com)
Slack (example: #sales)
Webhook (examples: CRM, Reporting System, Custom Application)
In-App (visible within the Web Application and Mobile Application)
Extension Events
Missed Call — triggered when a call reaches an Extension, nobody answers, and the caller disconnects. Channels: Email, Slack, Webhook, In-App.
Voicemail Received — triggered when a voicemail is created. Channels: Email, Slack, Webhook, In-App.
Transcript Ready — triggered when transcript generation completes. Channels: Email, Slack, Webhook, In-App.
Summary Ready — triggered when summary generation completes. Channels: Email, Slack, Webhook, In-App.
Shared SMS Inbox Events
New SMS — Channels: Email, Slack, Webhook, In-App. SMS Notifications belong to the inbox, not Extensions.
Multiple Destinations
A single event may notify multiple destinations.
Example: The Sales Extension notifies Email, Slack, and Webhook — all receive the event.
Email Behavior
Voicemail emails contain the Transcript and Summary inline, so no login is required to understand the message. Missed Call emails include Caller Number, Caller Name (if available), Extension, and Date/Time.
Slack Behavior
Slack messages are human-readable.
Example: New Voicemail Caller: 617-555-1111 Extension: Sales Summary: Customer requesting pricing information.
Webhook Behavior
Webhooks are event-driven. Supported events: Missed Call, Voicemail Created, Call Completed, Transcript Ready, SMS Received.
User Preferences
Users may control Personal Email Notifications and Personal In-App Notifications for Extensions they have access to. Users may not modify Business Slack Destinations, Business Webhooks, or Business Notification Rules.
Administrator Control
Administrators configure Slack Destinations, Webhooks, and Notification Rules. These remain authoritative.
Failure Handling
Email Failure — retried automatically.
Slack Failure — retried automatically; if failures persist, “Slack Connection Needs Attention.”
Webhook Failure — retried automatically; failures logged; if failures persist, the webhook is marked “Needs Attention.”
Search
Notifications are not a searchable system of record. Search occurs against Calls, Voicemails, and SMS — not notification history.
Audit Log
Recorded: Slack Destination Changed, Webhook Added, Webhook Removed, Email Recipient Changed.
Future Enhancements
Not V1: Notification Digests, Escalation Rules, SMS Notifications, Push Notification Rules, Conditional Notifications, AI Prioritization.
Feature: Shared SMS Inbox
Purpose
The Shared SMS Inbox provides a single place for a business to manage all SMS conversations across all Business Numbers. SMS belongs to the business and does not belong to Extensions, Users, or Business Numbers. All SMS-enabled Business Numbers feed into one Shared SMS Inbox.
User Experience
Messages received on any Business Number (examples: Main Number, Marketing Number, Google Ads Number, Support Number) all appear inside the Shared SMS Inbox. Users do not switch inboxes; Users may filter by Business Number.
Ownership
SMS belongs to the business, not to an Extension, User, or Business Number. Business Numbers act as filters only.
Conversations
Conversations are grouped by sender phone number (example: Jane Smith 617-555-1111). If a sender texts multiple Business Numbers, the conversation remains unified; Business Numbers remain visible for filtering and auditing.
Supported Content
SMS — supported.
MMS — supported (examples: Images, Photos, PDFs, Screenshots).
Views
Active Conversations — default view.
Archived Conversations — separate view.
Filter By Number (examples: Main Number, Marketing Number, Google Ads Number) — filters only, not separate inboxes.
Conversation Actions
Reply, Archive, Delete Conversation, Block Number.
Archive Behavior
Archived conversations remain searchable. If a new message arrives, the archived conversation automatically returns to Active Conversations.
Delete Conversation
Delete exists primarily for spam cleanup. Deleting a conversation permanently removes Messages, Attachments, and Conversation History. Confirmation is required.
Blocked Numbers
Blocked numbers cannot Call, Text, or Leave Voicemail. Existing history remains searchable, accessible, and visible. Blocking only affects future communication.
Caller Information
V1 is not a CRM and does not include Contact Management, Contact Import, Lead Status, or Tags. The inbox may display a Caller Name and Phone Number when available through caller identification services.
Notifications
A New SMS event notifies via Email, Slack, Webhook, and In-App. SMS Notifications belong to the Shared SMS Inbox, not to Extensions. (See Feature: Notifications.)
Permissions
Users either have Inbox Access or do not. V1 does not support granular inbox permissions. Inbox access is separate from Extension access. Users with inbox access may Read Messages, Reply, Archive Conversations, Delete Conversations, and Block Numbers.
Search
Search supports Phone Number, Caller Name (when available), SMS Content, Attachments, and Business Number. Search is global; users do not need to understand where information is stored.
Future Enhancements
Not V1: Assignment, Internal Notes, Tags, Ticket Workflow, Open/Closed Status, CRM Contact Management, Campaign SMS, Marketing Automation, AI Reply Suggestions, Shared Drafts, Future CRM/Patient/Customer Matching.
Feature: Search
Purpose
Search allows users to find information without needing to know where it is stored. Users should not need to remember which Extension handled a call, which Business Number received an SMS, whether something was a voicemail or a call, or whether a record is active or historical. Search should simply find it.
User Experience
Search is globally available throughout JOEL. Users may enter a Phone Number, Extension Name, Business Number, SMS Content, Voicemail Content, or Caller Name (when available) and receive relevant results.
Search Scope
Calls — Answered Calls, Missed Calls, Recorded Calls.
Voicemails — Transcript Content, Summary Content, Metadata.
SMS Conversations — Message Content, Attachments, Sender Numbers.

Business Numbers (examples: Main Number, Google Ads Number, Marketing Number).

Search behaves like modern software (examples: Spotlight, Gmail, Slack, Discord). Users should not need separate search modes. Search should feel immediate and users should not be required to choose Call Search, SMS Search, or Voicemail Search before searching.
Search Examples
Phone Number search (example: 6175551111) returns associated Calls, Voicemails, and SMS Conversations.

SMS search supports Message Content, Phone Number, and Caller Name when available (example: “appointment”).

Search results automatically respect permissions. Users only see information they are authorized to access.

Search includes historical records such as Deleted Extensions and Deleted Business Numbers, which remain searchable.

Feature: Users & Permissions
Purpose
Users provide access to JOEL. Users are permission containers. Users are not Extensions, Routing objects, Devices, or Business Numbers. Users exist to access the Web Application, access the Mobile Application, access the Shared SMS Inbox, access Extensions, and place outbound calls.
User Experience
Business owners invite Users (example: Jane jane@example.com). The User receives an invitation; after accepting, Web access is granted, Mobile access is granted, and permissions are applied.
User Types
Owner
Full access.
Admin
Operational administrator with full access to Business Numbers, Extensions, Devices, Users, Integrations, and the Shared SMS Inbox. Admins automatically have access to all Extensions.
User
Standard user. Access is controlled by permissions.
Multiple Businesses
Users may belong to multiple businesses (examples: Smilebar, Harlo, Personal Business). Permissions are independent for each business.
Extension Access
Extensions are the primary permission boundary.
Example: Sales granted, Support granted, Billing not granted.
If a User has access to an Extension they automatically receive access to Calls, Voicemails, Recordings, Forwarding Configuration, Notifications, and Outbound Calling. No separate per-feature permissions exist in V1.
Inbox Access
Inbox access is separate from Extension access.
Example: Inbox granted, Sales not granted is valid. A User may manage SMS without Extension access.
Outbound Calling Permissions
Users may place outbound calls only through Extensions they have access to.
Example: Sales granted allows outbound calls via Sales; Support not granted does not.
Caller ID Permissions
Users may only use caller IDs allowed for the Extension and allowed by administrator configuration.
Example: Sales Extension allowed caller IDs — Sales Number, Main Number. The User may not use Support Number or Billing Number unless permitted.
Permission Changes
If permissions are removed, the User immediately loses access after refresh. If actively viewing restricted content, an “Access Removed” message is displayed and the User is returned to a valid screen.
User Removal
Removing a User revokes permissions, revokes Mobile access, revokes Web access, and logs out active sessions. Business data remains intact.
Notifications
Users may control Personal Email Notifications and Personal In-App Notifications for Extensions they have access to. Users may not modify Business Slack Destinations, Business Webhooks, or Business Notification Rules; administrator-configured destinations remain authoritative.
Mobile Application Access
Users may install and use the Mobile Application. (See Feature: Mobile Application.)
Search
Users may search Calls, Voicemails, SMS, Extensions, and Business Numbers, subject to permissions. Search results are automatically filtered; Users never see data they do not have access to.
Audit Log
Recorded: User Invited, User Removed, Role Changed, Extension Access Changed, Inbox Access Changed.
Future Enhancements
Not V1: View-Only Permissions, Per-Feature Permissions, Temporary Access, Team-Based Permissions, SSO, SCIM Provisioning.

Feature: Validation Rules
Validation rules enforce structural integrity. They are organized by object. These rules restate constraints already defined throughout this document; no new behavior is introduced.
Extensions
Extension numbers must be unique within a business.
Extension 0 is reserved for Operator.
Extension 9 is reserved for Directory.
Standard Extensions are 1 through 8; additional Extensions begin at 100.
Extension creation requires an Extension Number, Extension Name, Text-To-Voice Name, and Initial Forwarding Number.
Menu entries are created and removed automatically with Extensions; broken menu entries are never left behind.
Disabled and Deleted Extensions do not appear in the Directory.
Business Numbers
Business Numbers must be unique.
A Business Number routes only to a Greeting/Menu or a single Extension; it never routes directly to a Phone Number, SIP Phone, or Mobile Application.
Outbound caller ID must always be a business-owned Business Number.
A temporary number is always assigned during porting and cannot be skipped.
Deleted Business Numbers enter a recovery window and are not immediately released; reuse is only possible after the recovery period expires.
Users
User email addresses must be unique within a business.
A User may belong to multiple businesses; permissions are independent per business.
Inbox Access is independent of Extension access.
Users may place outbound calls only through Extensions they have access to, and may only use caller IDs allowed for the Extension.
Destinations
A Destination must be a Phone Number, a SIP Phone, or a Mobile Application.
A Mobile Application Destination requires an existing User.
A SIP Phone or Mobile Application may be attached to multiple Extensions.
Disabled Destinations behave as though they do not exist.
Order applies only to Ring One At A Time routing.
Routing
Routing belongs to the Extension; it cannot be owned by a Business Number, Device, or User.
Routing loops are prohibited; JOEL prevents saving invalid configurations.
A routing method must be Ring All At Once or Ring One At A Time.
Fallback behavior must be Voicemail, Disconnect, or Forward To Extension.
Integrations
Webhook URLs must be valid HTTPS URLs.
Slack Destinations, Webhooks, and Notification Rules are administrator-configured and authoritative; Users cannot modify business destinations.

Feature: Business Lifecycle
Purpose

Active, Cancelled, Deleted, Restored.
Cancellation
When a customer cancels, the account remains active through the end of the billing cycle: Business Numbers continue routing, SMS continues functioning, Extensions continue functioning, and Users retain access. Cancellation does not immediately disable service.
End Of Billing Cycle
At billing expiration, Business Numbers stop routing, SMS stops functioning, User access is disabled, and data is retained during the recovery period.
Deletion
Deleting a business requires confirmation. (Future MFA confirmation may be supported.) Deletion is a soft delete; the business enters a 30-Day Recovery Window.
Recovery Window
During recovery, the business may be restored. Restoration restores Business Numbers, Extensions, Routing, Greetings, SMS, Users, Devices, Integrations, and Audit History. No reconfiguration is required.
Permanent Deletion
After the recovery period expires, business data becomes eligible for permanent deletion. Legal preservation requirements may override deletion.
Number Protection
Business Numbers are never immediately released; the recovery period always applies first.
Historical Records
Deleted businesses retain Calls, Voicemails, SMS, Recordings, and Audit History throughout the recovery period.
Audit Log
Recorded: Business Cancelled, Business Restored, Business Deleted.
Future Enhancements
Not V1: Export Before Deletion, Compliance Retention Overrides, Self-Service Legal Hold.
Feature: Billing & Plan Enforcement
Purpose
Defines system behavior when customers reach plan limits or attempt to use unavailable features. This section defines system behavior; it does not define pricing.
Upgrade Required
When a customer attempts to use a feature unavailable on their current plan, JOEL displays “Upgrade Required” with an explanation.
Example: On Lite, attempting to add a SIP Phone responds “Upgrade To Grow.” On Grow, attempting to add a Webhook responds “Upgrade To Max.”
Add-Ons
Supported add-ons: Additional Numbers, and SIP Phones (Grow and above). Add-ons do not require plan upgrades. Additional Business Numbers may be purchased without changing plans; number purchases remain separate from plan upgrades.
SIP Phone Add-On Behavior
SIP Phone functionality is available only on qualifying plans. Attempting to add a SIP Phone on an unsupported plan displays “Upgrade Required.”
Upgrades
Upgrades are immediate and prorated.
Downgrades
Downgrades are effective at next renewal and do not immediately disable existing functionality.
Downgrade Warning
Before a downgrade, the customer sees Features Lost, Disabled Integrations, Recording Changes, and Permission Changes. Confirmation is required.
Plan Limits
When a customer reaches a plan limit, JOEL displays Upgrade Plan. There are no hidden overages and no surprise billing.
Existing Features
Features already configured continue functioning until a downgrade becomes effective. No mid-cycle feature removal occurs.
Add-On Removal
Removing an add-on (example: an Additional Number) takes effect at the end of the billing cycle. The customer receives a warning before removal.
Audit Log
Recorded: Plan Changed, Upgrade, Downgrade, Add-On Purchased, Add-On Removed.
Future Enhancements
Not V1: Usage-Based Billing, Overage Billing, Automatic Plan Recommendations, AI Cost Optimization.
Feature: Error States & Recovery
Purpose
Ensure failures are understandable, recoverable, and human-readable. Customers should never see telecom terminology.
Error Philosophy
Customer-facing messages replace telecom terminology with plain language.
Example: Instead of “SIP Registration Failed,” show “Your desk phone is offline.” Instead of “Carrier Provisioning Error,” show “We couldn’t purchase that number. Please try another number.”
(The customer-facing phrase “desk phone” is intentional plain language; the product term for the device remains SIP Phone.)
Number Purchase Failure
The customer may Retry or Select Another Number. No telecom details are exposed.
Port Failure
The customer sees “Additional Information Required” or “Carrier Rejected Request,” with next steps.
Slack Failure
Retried automatically. If failures persist, “Slack Connection Needs Attention.”
Webhook Failure
Retried automatically. Failures are logged. If failures continue, the webhook is marked “Needs Attention.”
Device Failure
A plain-language message such as “Desk Phone Offline” is shown instead of SIP terminology.
Recording Failure
Calls continue. Communication never fails because recording failed.
Transcript Failure
The call and voicemail remain available. The transcript is marked “Unavailable” and the user may retry later.
SMS Failure
The conversation remains visible. Failed messages are clearly marked, and the user may retry.
Search Failure
If search indexing is unavailable, users receive “Search Temporarily Unavailable.” Core communication functions continue operating.
Recovery Philosophy
Whenever possible, JOEL preserves communication, preserves data, retries automatically, and explains clearly.
Future Enhancements
Not V1: Advanced Diagnostics, AI Recovery Suggestions, Automated Carrier Escalation.
Feature: Legal Preservation & Compliance Holds
Purpose
Allow legal and regulatory requirements to override standard retention and deletion behavior.
Legal Preservation Sources
Examples: Court Orders, Search Warrants, Subpoenas, Regulatory Requests, Legal Holds.
Effect
When legal preservation is active, normal retention policies may be suspended. Affected data may include Calls, Recordings, Voicemails, SMS, Audit Logs, and Account Data.
Customer Deletion Requests
Customer deletion requests do not override active legal preservation requirements.
Product Rule
Retention policies define normal behavior. Legal preservation requirements supersede retention policies when legally required.
Scope
Legal preservation may apply to an entire business, an individual Business Number, an individual Extension, a specific date range, or specific records, depending on legal requirements.
Audit Log
Recorded where legally permitted: Legal Hold Applied, Legal Hold Removed.
Future Enhancements
Not V1: Self-Service Legal Hold, Legal Hold Reporting, Compliance Export Packages, Regulatory Templates.
