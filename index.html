How it compares

The honest comparison

Most small businesses start with a personal cell phone or a second number. Then calls pile up, texts get scattered, and adding help makes the phone mess worse. Enterprise systems solve the problem by adding too much system. JOEL is the middle path: one business number, simple routing, shared texts, voicemail, and Slack notifications for teams of one to ten.

Option
What it means for a small business
Personal cell phone
Free and familiar, but every call hits one phone, your personal number gets passed around, and there is no menu, no shared texts, and no easy way to route calls to someone else.
Google Voice
Gives you a second number, but it is mostly built for one person. It can work early on, but it is not built around menus, extensions, shared team texting, or routing calls by function.
Enterprise phone systems
Genuinely capable - Slack and WhatsApp integrations, AI features, and deep routing are all there. But that power comes with per-seat pricing, contracts, and an admin console built for larger teams and call centers. Most small businesses pay for and configure far more system than they need just to answer calls and texts.
JOEL
One business number, a simple menu, calls routed by function, voicemail with transcripts, Slack notifications, and a shared text inbox. Set up in one sitting, built for a team of one to ten.
